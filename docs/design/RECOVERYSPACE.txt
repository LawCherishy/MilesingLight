RECOVERY CONCEPT SPACE

PURPOSE
Opening the app finds the strongest complete local authored body and makes every recovery choice understandable before changing files.

DIRECT RECOVERY FLOW
- Inspect the selected final work, protected prior body, and known sibling candidates by header first.
- Parse each body under strict byte, item, recursion, expansion, and duration ceilings.
- Verify chunk and manifest digests.
- Portray each complete candidate with generation, write time, logical root, authored-work summary, and exact path.
- Prefer no body silently: show the current final first and make a stronger recoverable body visible as a deliberate choice.
- Open a chosen body read-only before promoting it.
- Promotion writes and verifies a new candidate, then follows the replacement flow.
- Preserve the original recovery bodies until the promoted result has reopened successfully.
- A missing render cache, search index, generated geometry body, or decoded image never damages authored work; regenerate it.
- A recipe that depended on an expired non-derivable hardware sample portrays that dependency and opens a fresh present encounter only through a new exact grant.
- Recovery commands are keyboard reachable and announced through the same semantic command model.

CODE HOME
SUPPORT.txt owns inspection, bounded parsing, comparison, and promotion. EDITOR.txt owns the visible recovery workspace. APP.txt owns explicit invocation and window messaging.
